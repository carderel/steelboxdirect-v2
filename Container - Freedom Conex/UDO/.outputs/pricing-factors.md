## Why We Don't List Prices

Container prices vary significantly based on factors we can't know until we talk to you: your location, what's available nearby, current market conditions, and delivery complexity.

Publishing fixed prices would either mislead you (if outdated) or anchor you to numbers that don't reflect your situation.

Instead, this page explains **what drives costs** so you can evaluate quotes intelligently.

---

## The Four Price Drivers

### 1. Size

Larger containers cost more. This is the most predictable factor.

| Size | Relative Cost |
|------|---------------|
| 20-foot | Base |
| 40-foot | ~40-60% more than 20-foot |
| 40-foot High Cube | Slightly more than standard 40-foot |

The per-square-foot cost actually decreases as you go larger. A 40-foot container is not double the price of a 20-foot.

---

### 2. Condition

Condition has the biggest impact on price after size.

| Condition | Relative Cost | What You Get |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| New (One-Trip) | Highest | Like-new appearance, minimal wear |
| Cargo Worthy | Mid-range | Structurally sound, cosmetic wear |
| Wind & Water Tight | Lower | Weatherproof, more visible wear |
| As-Is | Lowest | Variable condition, may need work |

**The spread is significant.** A cargo-worthy used container might cost 40-60% less than a new one-trip container of the same size.

For most storage applications, cargo-worthy offers the best value: reliable function without paying for appearance.

See our [Condition Guide →] for details on each grade.

---

### 3. Delivery Distance and Complexity

Delivery is often a larger cost factor than buyers expect.

**Distance matters:**
- Containers ship from depot locations
- The farther from a depot, the higher the delivery cost
- Our service area covers approximately 250 miles from Cincinnati, Ohio

**Site complexity matters:**
- Easy access (straight driveway, firm ground) = standard delivery
- Tight access, soft ground, or obstacles = may require special equipment
- Crane placement adds cost but enables difficult locations

**What you can control:**
- Know your site conditions before requesting a quote
- Clear obstacles and prepare ground if possible
- Be flexible on exact placement if access is tight

See our [Delivery Guide →] for access requirements.

---

### 4. Market Timing and Availability

Container prices fluctuate based on supply and demand.

**Factors that affect availability:**
- Shipping industry cycles (imports/exports affect supply)
- Seasonal demand (construction season, agricultural cycles)
- Regional inventory levels
- Current condition mix at nearby depots

**What this means for you:**
- Prices are not fixed—they reflect current market
- Specific conditions or sizes may have limited availability
- Flexibility on condition or timing can sometimes reduce cost

We can't control market conditions, but we can tell you what's actually available and priced fairly.

## What's Included vs. Extra

### Typically Included:
- The container itself
- Standard delivery to your site
- Basic placement

### Often Extra:
- Crane delivery (if standard tilt-bed won't work)
- Ground preparation
- Blocking/leveling materials
- Modifications (doors, vents, paint, insulation)

**Ask what's included.** A lower container price with expensive delivery extras may cost more than a slightly higher all-in quote.

---

## How to Evaluate a Quote

When you receive a quote, check:

1. **What's the total delivered price?**
   - Container + delivery + any extras
   - Compare apples to apples across quotes

2. **What condition is specified?**
   - "Used" is vague—cargo worthy, WWT, or as-is?
   - Get specific grade confirmation

3. **What's the delivery timeline?**
   - When will it arrive?
   - Is the timeline guaranteed or estimated?

4. **What happens if there's a problem?**
   - What if the container doesn't match description?
   - What's the process for issues?
